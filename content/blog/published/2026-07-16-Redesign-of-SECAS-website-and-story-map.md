As you’ve probably noticed, the SECAS website has a fresh look and feel! After 10 ten years of loyal service, it was time to migrate our site to more modern web technology so that it will continue to serve us well for another 10. If the new look reminds you a bit of the [Blueprint Explorer](https://apps.fws.gov/southeastblueprint/), that’s no accident—big thanks to Brendan Ward at [Astute Spruce](https://astutespruce.com/) for his extensive work on the redesign!  

<!--more-->  

Updating the underlying software framework also provided the perfect excuse to refresh the content and streamline the flow of information throughout the site. Whether you’re learning about SECAS for the first time, or you’re a long-time partner looking for the latest Blueprint data, we want to make it easy to find the information you’re looking for.  

So, what’s new? I don’t want to bore you with a thorough recounting of every tweak, but here are some of the more significant improvements: 

- The top navigation bar now functions as a dropdown menu, so you can more easily find links that were buried in text in the previous site, particularly under "Blueprint"!

- Under "About", you'll now find information about the partnership that was previously spread out across various parts of the site. This includes the SECAS Goal and the staff page. A new page dedicated to teams and committees frees up the partners page to focus on grassroots involvement in SECAS. 

- Overall, the site is now much more mobile-friendly with a design that should seamlessly rescale to any screen size. 

- The [story map](/story-map/) has also gotten a facelift! Now each story has a unique url so that you can share links to specific stories, instead of the entire map. The pins for each story also now change color after you click on them, much like hyperlinks do, so you can see which ones you’ve looked at before. 

What do you think? As always, if America runs on Dunkin’, SECAS runs on user feedback—so please pass on any suggestions to me at [hilary_morris@fws.gov](mailto:hilary_morris@fws.gov). Especially if you're having trouble finding something or run into a bug or even a pesky typo, I’d really appreciate you letting me know so we can address it.
